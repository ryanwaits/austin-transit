# PRODUCT

register: brand

## Product purpose

A public-facing interactive **visual essay** site that independently measures
CapMetro (Austin) transit reliability and tells the story of who bears the cost
when on-time promises slip. Data journalism, not a dashboard. Each essay is a
scrollytelling argument backed by an independent measurement pipeline.

## Users

General public, Austin riders, transit advocates, journalists, and city
policy staff. They arrive from a link, read top-to-bottom on desktop (mobile
usable), and leave persuaded by evidence, not dazzled by controls. They are
not operating a tool; they are reading.

## Brand & tone

Editorial, rigorous, quietly confident. The credibility comes from restraint
and from showing our work (methodology, disclaimers, confidence intervals), not
from hype. Warm, humane, print-adjacent. The signature is a warm cream page
that reads like a well-set magazine, not a SaaS app.

- Voice: measured, specific, honest about limits. Names real routes and
  neighborhoods. Never editorializes past what one week of data supports.
- The "What we're not saying" rigor section is mandatory and load-bearing for
  trust. It is what separates this from a hot-take blog.

## Anti-references (what we are NOT)

- Not a dashboard: no filters, global controls, or "build your own view."
- Not a Tailwind-default SaaS landing page. No hero-metric template, no
  identical card grids, no gradient accents.
- Not dark mode. The cream background is the brand; there is no dark theme.
- Not emoji-driven. No emoji anywhere in the UI.
- Reflex-reject: generic "civic-tech" blue, govtech gray, chartjs dashboards.

## Strategic principles

1. Editorial first. The prose column leads; visualizations serve the argument.
2. Show the work. Sources under every chart, methodology linked, CIs visible.
3. One color encodes one variable per chart, ever. Direct labels over legends.
4. Works without JS for the static layer; interactivity is enhancement.
5. Reusable primitives so future essays ship faster (one essay this sprint).

## Reference aesthetic

The Pudding (pudding.cool) — editorial data journalism. Narrow centered prose
column with full-bleed visualizations that break out wider at key moments.
